Glass Modeling Pipeline

1

Reference Analysis & Proportions

I began by studying high-quality wine glasses, analyzing their proportions, curvature, and structural elements. Using calipers and reference images, I established precise measurements for the bowl, stem, and base to ensure authentic proportions.

2

Curve Modeling & Surface Refinement

The elegant curves of the wine glass required careful attention to continuity and smoothness. I used Bezier curves and subdivision surface techniques to create the perfect silhouette, focusing on maintaining consistent wall thickness throughout the model.

üîß Advanced Technique: Liquid Simulation

I created a realistic wine simulation using fluid dynamics, capturing the meniscus effect and surface tension that occurs when liquid meets glass.

3

Glass Material Development

Creating realistic glass required a multi-layered approach. The material includes:

Glass Body

Clear crystal with IOR of 1.5

Wine Liquid

Deep red with light absorption

Surface Imperfections

Micro-scratches and fingerprints

4

Lighting & Rendering Optimization

Glass rendering requires specialized lighting setups. I implemented:

  • Three-point lighting with emphasis on backlighting
  • HDRI environment maps for realistic reflections
  • Caustics calculation for light focusing effects
  • Denoising for clean, noise-free renders
