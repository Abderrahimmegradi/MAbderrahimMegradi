My Blender Workflow

1

Reference Collection & Blueprint Setup

I began by gathering extensive reference images of mid-century modern furniture. Using Blender's background images feature, I set up blueprints from multiple angles to ensure accurate proportions. This foundation is crucial for creating believable furniture that follows real-world design principles.

üí° Pro Tip

Always collect references from multiple angles and lighting conditions. I use PureRef to organize my reference boards and keep them visible throughout the modeling process.

2

Blocking & Primary Forms

Starting with primitive cubes and cylinders, I blocked out the major shapes focusing on overall proportions. At this stage, I paid special attention to the table's ergonomics and real-world dimensions, ensuring it would feel natural in a virtual environment.

Initial Blockout

Proportion Check

3

Detailed Modeling & Edge Flow

Using subdivision surface modeling techniques, I refined the basic shapes into detailed forms. I focused on creating clean edge loops around curved areas and beveled sharp edges to catch realistic highlights. The table legs received particular attention with proper edge flow for potential deformation.

üéØ Key Technique: Subdivision Surface

I used supporting edge loops to maintain sharp corners while benefiting from subdivision smoothing. This approach allows for high-quality results with relatively low polycount.

4

UV Unwrapping & Texture Planning

I carefully unwrapped the model using Blender's UV editing tools, ensuring minimal stretching and efficient texture space usage. Strategic seams were placed in less visible areas, and I utilized UV grids to verify distortion-free mapping.

5

Material Creation & Shader Development

Using Blender's node editor, I created a complex wood material with layered imperfections. The shader includes:

  • Procedural wood grain with controllable direction and intensity
  • Wear maps for edges and high-contact areas
  • Micro-surface variations for realistic light scattering
  • Clear coat layer for finished wood appearance
6

Lighting & Rendering Setup

I implemented a three-point lighting system with an HDRI environment for natural reflections. Using Cycles render engine with optimized sample settings, I achieved photorealistic results while maintaining reasonable render times.

‚ö° Performance Optimization

By using adaptive subdivision and denoising, I reduced render times by 40% without sacrificing quality. The final scene renders in under 5 minutes at 4K resolution.

7

Export & Real-time Optimization

For web display, I optimized the model by:

  • Reducing polygon count while preserving visual quality
  • Baking high-poly details into normal maps
  • Optimizing texture sizes and formats
  • Setting up LOD (Level of Detail) versions
