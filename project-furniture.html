Furniture Modeling Pipeline

1

Reference Collection & Blueprint Setup

I began by gathering extensive reference images of mid-century modern furniture, focusing on iconic designs with tapered legs and organic tabletop shapes. Using Blender's background images feature, I set up precise blueprints from multiple angles to ensure accurate proportions and authentic design language.

üí° Design Research

Studied furniture design principles including ergonomic heights, stable base proportions, and visual weight distribution to create a table that feels both aesthetically pleasing and functionally sound.

2

Blocking & Primary Forms

Starting with primitive cubes and cylinders, I blocked out the major shapes focusing on overall proportions and silhouette. Special attention was paid to the elegant taper of the legs and the subtle curvature of the tabletop edges that characterize mid-century design.

3

Detailed Modeling & Edge Flow

Using subdivision surface modeling techniques, I refined the basic shapes into detailed forms with clean edge loops around curved areas. The table legs received particular attention with proper edge flow to support the elegant taper, while the tabletop maintained consistent thickness with subtle edge detailing.

üéØ Key Technique: Subdivision Surface

Implemented supporting edge loops to maintain sharp corners while benefiting from subdivision smoothing, creating high-quality curved surfaces with relatively low polycount and optimal render performance.

4

UV Unwrapping & Texture Planning

I carefully unwrapped the model using Blender's UV editing tools, ensuring minimal stretching and efficient texture space usage. Strategic seams were placed along natural panel divisions and less visible areas, with careful attention to wood grain direction continuity across surfaces.

5

Material Creation & Shader Development

Using Blender's node editor and Substance Painter, I created a complex wood material system with layered imperfections and realistic surface properties. The shader includes:

  • Procedural wood grain with controllable direction and intensity
  • Wear maps for edges and high-contact areas
  • Micro-surface variations for realistic light scattering
  • Clear coat layer with adjustable sheen for finished wood appearance
  • Subtle color variations mimicking natural wood characteristics
6

Lighting & Rendering Setup

I implemented a professional studio lighting system with an HDRI environment for natural reflections and soft shadows. Using Cycles render engine with optimized sample settings, I achieved photorealistic results that showcase the wood material's depth and character while maintaining reasonable render times.

‚ö° Performance Optimization

By using adaptive sampling, light path optimization, and AI denoising, I reduced render times by 40% without sacrificing quality, achieving production-ready results in under 5 minutes at 4K resolution.

7

Export & Real-time Optimization

For web display and real-time applications, I optimized the model through several techniques:

  • Reduced polygon count while preserving visual quality through retopology
  • Baked high-poly details into normal and ambient occlusion maps
  • Optimized texture sizes and formats for web delivery
  • Created LOD (Level of Detail) versions for performance scaling
  • Set up efficient material groups and naming conventions
