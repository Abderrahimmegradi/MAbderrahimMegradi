Project Overview

This project represents my passion for automotive design and hard-surface modeling. The 1970 Chevrolet Camaro Pro Touring edition was meticulously recreated with attention to every detail, from the body curves to the engine components.

  • Modular component system for easy customization
  • Realistic lighting setup with car paint specific shaders

Automotive Modeling Pipeline

1

Blueprint Analysis & Scale Setup

I began with extensive research, collecting factory blueprints, reference photos, and technical specifications. Using Blender's background images, I set up orthographic views to ensure millimeter-perfect accuracy in proportions and dimensions.

2

Body Panel Modeling

The complex curves of the Camaro body required advanced subdivision surface techniques. I focused on maintaining continuous curvature while ensuring clean topology for potential animation and deformation.

üîß Advanced Technique: Panel Gaps

I used boolean operations with precise beveling to create realistic panel gaps between body components, a crucial detail for automotive realism.

7

Car Paint Shader Development

The most challenging aspect was creating a realistic car paint material. My solution involved:

  • Base color with metallic flake pattern
  • Clear coat layer with Fresnel reflection
  • Orange peel effect for surface imperfection
  • Environment reflection mapping
