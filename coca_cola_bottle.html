Bottle Modeling Pipeline

1

Reference Analysis & Proportions

I began by studying the iconic Coca-Cola contour bottle, analyzing its unique curves, proportions, and structural elements. Using reference images and physical measurements, I established precise dimensions for the bottle body, neck, and cap to ensure authentic reproduction of this classic design.

Bottle Volume

Standard 330ml beverage container size

Contour Shape

Iconic curved silhouette recognition

Base Stability

Proper weight distribution and balance

2

Curve Modeling & Surface Refinement

The distinctive curves of the Coca-Cola bottle required careful attention to continuity and smoothness. I used Bezier curves and subdivision surface techniques to create the perfect silhouette, focusing on maintaining consistent glass thickness throughout the model for realistic light behavior.

ðŸ”§ Advanced Technique: Subdivision Modeling

Implemented subdivision surface modeling with supporting edge loops to maintain sharp edges while benefiting from smooth curvature, creating a perfect balance between detail and performance.

3

Glass Material & Condensation Development

Creating realistic glass with condensation effects required a multi-layered approach with attention to physical properties. The material system includes complex node setups for accurate light behavior and surface wetness.

  • Glass body with IOR of 1.5 for soda-lime glass
  • Procedural condensation with droplet formation
  • Brand label with accurate typography and colors
  • Metallic cap with threading details
4

Lighting & Rendering Optimization

Glass rendering requires specialized lighting setups to showcase transparency and refraction. I implemented a studio lighting environment specifically designed for transparent materials, with careful attention to backlighting and reflection management.
