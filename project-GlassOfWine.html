Glass Modeling Pipeline

1

Reference Analysis & Proportions

I began by studying high-quality crystal wine glasses, analyzing their proportions, curvature, and structural elements. Using calipers and reference images, I established precise measurements for the bowl, stem, and base to ensure authentic proportions that reflect premium glassware design.

Bowl Volume

Standard 12-16 oz capacity for red wine

Stem Length

Optimal 3-4 inch stem for proper handling

Base Diameter

Stable 2.5-3 inch base for balance

2

Curve Modeling & Surface Refinement

The elegant curves of the wine glass required careful attention to continuity and smoothness. I used Bezier curves and subdivision surface techniques to create the perfect silhouette, focusing on maintaining consistent wall thickness throughout the model for realistic light behavior.

üîß Advanced Technique: Subdivision Modeling

Implemented subdivision surface modeling with supporting edge loops to maintain sharp edges while benefiting from smooth curvature, creating a perfect balance between detail and performance.

3

Glass Material Development

Creating realistic glass required a multi-layered approach with attention to physical properties. The material system includes complex node setups for accurate light behavior and surface properties.

  • Glass body with IOR of 1.5 for crystal
  • Surface imperfections and micro-scratches
  • Thin-film interference for iridescent effects
  • Caustics simulation for light focusing
4

Lighting & Rendering Optimization

Glass rendering requires specialized lighting setups to showcase transparency and refraction. I implemented a studio lighting environment specifically designed for transparent materials, with careful attention to backlighting and reflection management.

HDRI Setup
Environment Lighting - Studio HDRI for soft, realistic reflections
Backlight
Rim Lighting - Accent lights to highlight glass edges
Fill Lights
Soft Illumination - Subtle fill lights to reduce harsh shadows
Render Passes
Multi-pass Rendering - Separate passes for refraction, reflection, and specular
5

Post-Processing & Final Output

The final stage involved compositing render passes, color grading, and preparing the model for various output formats including web display, product visualization, and architectural integration.
